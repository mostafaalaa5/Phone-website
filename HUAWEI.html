Introduction

Huawei Technologies Co., Ltd. (/ˈhwɑːˌweɪ/, simplified Chinese 华为技术有限公司) (pronounced Huáwéi, (Huawei)) is a Chinese manufacturer of telecommunications equipment and consumer electronics, headquartered in Shenzhen, Guangdong , China.

The company was founded in 1987 by Ren Zhengfei. Initially, Huawei focused on manufacturing telephone boards and switches, and later expanded its business to include building telecommunications networks, providing operational and consulting services and equipment for enterprises inside and outside China, and manufacturing communications devices for the consumer market. Huawei had more than 170,000 employees as of September 2017, about 76,000 of whom are engaged in research and development (R&D). It has 21 research and development institutes around the world. As of 2017, the company has invested $13.8 billion in research and development
